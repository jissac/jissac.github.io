Learning to Fall

 Posted on January 1, 1018  |  James Issac

I went skiing for the first time at Mt. Brighton (pictured above). Now seasoned skiers might chuckle at these rounded slopes, but for a first-timer like me, these hills looked like the Himalayan peaks. And indeed, although I had watched countless YouTube videos about skiing the week before, the moment I buckled up the rented boots to the skis, my meticulous research and mental preparation melted away.

I signed up for beginners lessons with my group, and after learning the basics, we proceeded to ski down the beginner bunny slope. This was the moment I had mentally envisioned all week, gliding gracefully down mountains of snow. However, I still had to get on top of the bunny hill. Now to get up this slope, there was something called the ‘magic carpet’, which is an automated conveyor belt carrying beginners to the top. I patiently lined up behind the 5-year-olds jockeying to get in front. This was it. I closed my eyes and imagined the view from atop the bunny hill. I opened my eyes just in time to see that I was next in line. Next thing I knew, I slid onto the magic carpet, lost my balance, and saw the laughing blue sky above me. Yep, it was pretty embarrassing.

Well, after such an accomplishment, how could I quit? And indeed, I learned how to fall a thousand different ways throughout the course of the day, finally graduating from the dreaded bunny hill to some of the more difficult slopes. I think I just needed to get used to the fact that I was traveling fast, to get over my fear of this unfamiliar experience. And though I fell in the silliest of ways, I tried not to let that get me down. After all, according to my buddy T. Edison, “I have not failed. I’ve just found 10,000 ways that won’t work.”

Therefore, in the spirit of discovery and trying new things, we must always learn from our experiences and never fear failure. If the recent popularity of the game Flappy Birds has taught me anything, it’s that sometimes, in order to go further we must learn how to fall.
